Tata Selo

Isinulat ni: Rogelio R. Sikat

February 09, 2024
by Nathaniel A. Gomez
MEXE-4201

In our world, people are divided based on their social status, with those who are more well-off often looking down on those who have less. Money is seen as the most important factor in determining who gets what in life. Those who have more wealth usually have more possessions, like houses and land, as well as things like happiness, safety, loyalty, and fairness.

The story of Tata Selo by Rogelio Sikat tells of a man who feels trapped and powerless, like a bird stuck in a cage. But unlike a bird, he's judged and punished by others. In the story, Tata Selo is wrongly accused of murder and sentenced to death without a fair trial. It's clear that justice is not served because Tata Selo might have acted in self-defense or revenge, yet he isn't given a chance to explain his side.

In this system, where money talks, justice is distorted. While Tata Selo might have made mistakes, it's important to look at the reasons behind his actions. Punishment should be fair and based on all the facts, and everyone involved should be held accountable for what they've done. Justice should be the same for everyone, no matter how rich or poor they are.
